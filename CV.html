Design Projects

University Capstone Project (2018)

Our capstone team worked with the Alberta Children's Hospital Research Institute to build an application for the MinIon, a hand-held DNA sequencer. Our algorithm identified human DNA in real-time - and then throws it out! What's left is invasive (such as virus, or viral) DNA. This information could be used to help scientists identify the strain and characteristics of a virus; resulting in information that can estimate the spread of the virus and expedite the development of a vaccine.

TOM Makeathon (2015)

Participated in Calgary's first TOM as the electrical lead. Teams worked with someone with a disability and created a solution to their lifestyle in 72 hrs.

  • Served as electrical lead and designer using Arduino.
  • Developed a tactile obstacle detection attachment to common "seeing sticks", using sonar sensors to detect obstacles and translate the distance into haptic vibrations in the handle. The interval of vibration corellated to the distance of the obstacle.
  • This solution is discreet for the user, and in addition to the way seeing sticks are commonly used, added another dimension of tactile obstacle detection.
